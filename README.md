## My Engineering Approach

Whenever I start a new project, I usually ask the same questions:

* What is the real problem?
* Who experiences this problem?
* Why is it painful?
* Can the workflow be simplified?
* Can repetitive work be automated?
* What are the engineering trade-offs?
* How can users stay in control?

Only after answering these questions do I decide which technologies to use.

---

## Engineering Principles

I try to follow a few simple principles in every project:

* Understand the problem before writing code.
* Technology is a tool, not the goal.
* Keep solutions as simple as possible.
* Design systems that are easy to maintain.
* Automate repetitive work whenever it makes sense.
* Accept trade-offs instead of chasing perfect solutions.
* Build software that creates measurable value.
